Projects

OpenQueensPark (in progress)

A civic-tech platform providing accessible, automated overviews of daily proceedings in the Ontario Legislature — modelled after the federal openparliament.ca. Scrapes Hansard transcripts via the OLA API, stores structured data in SQLite, and uses an on-device LLM (Ollama) to generate neutral, party-by-party summaries. Features a Streamlit front end with calendar navigation, word clouds, and a "Word of the Day" metric. Self-hosted on a Raspberry Pi and exposed via Cloudflare Tunnel at openqueenspark.ca.

  • Python
  • SQLite
  • Streamlit
  • Ollama (LLM)
  • Raspberry Pi

BasketWatch (in progress)

A Canadian grocery price tracker that constructs a shadow CPI by monitoring a consistent basket of staple items across major chains — Loblaws, Walmart, No Frills, Metro, and Sobeys. Weekly price data is scraped and stored over time, enabling cross-chain comparisons and divergence analysis against Statistics Canada's official CPI. Designed to answer whether discount and premium chains respond differently to inflationary pressures.

  • Python
  • BeautifulSoup
  • SQLite
  • Pandas
  • Streamlit

Canadian Housing Price Model (in progress)

An interactive multiple regression model exploring the drivers of Canadian housing prices. Combines variables rarely modelled together — immigration levels, federal and provincial government spending, tax policy, and oil & gas prices — into a unified, publicly accessible dashboard. Built to bridge existing siloed academic literature and give Canadians a transparent tool to explore what actually moves the market.

  • Python
  • scikit-learn
  • Pandas
  • StatsCan API
  • Plotly

Budget Pulse: Federal Rhetoric & Market Response (in progress)

A research-grade pipeline that fetches federal Hansard budget speeches, applies LLM-based sentiment and topic analysis, and correlates the results against TSX daily close data from Yahoo Finance. Explores lead-lag relationships between parliamentary economic rhetoric and equity market movement — bridging political science, economics, and NLP in a single reproducible workflow.

  • Python
  • Ollama (LLM)
  • yfinance
  • Pandas
  • Statsmodels
